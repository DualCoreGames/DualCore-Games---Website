Room Raider

Your physical room is the level. A deep-dive into the engineering and spatial AI behind a real-world Mixed Reality dodgeball experience.

01 / Overview

Redefining the Arena.

Room Raider transforms the player's physical room into a living game arena. Unlike traditional VR, this experience anchors digital gameplay directly to the player's environment.

The AI characters understand, navigate, and react to the real room, using furniture, walls, and objects as cover. This creates a uniquely personal and immersive dodgeball experience that feels grounded in reality.

"The game's core tension comes from not knowing where an enemy is hiding — it becomes genuinely exciting when the enemy is behind your actual couch."

02 / Engineering

System Architecture

Architected with a layered approach, Room Raider leverages the Meta XR SDK for hardware perception and custom Unity systems for high-performance gameplay logic.

  • RoomAnalyzer System Ray-casts against physical mesh to classify anchors (floor, walls, furniture) and build runtime spawn maps.
  • Hybrid Navigation A custom A* waypoint graph combined with local steering behaviors for natural AI movement without expensive NavMesh baking.
  • Physics Life-Cycle Optimized PhysX integration for dodgeball trajectories with controller-velocity-derived release delta.

Technical Challenges

Engineering Solutions

PROBLEM 01

Real-World Occlusion

Virtual objects traditionally render on top of Passthrough. We implemented Depth Masking via Meta Building Blocks to ensure enemies correctly hide behind real furniture.

PROBLEM 02

Unpredictable Geometry

Room meshes often have gaps. Our RoomAnalyzer applies a secondary validation pass, ray-casting to confirm bounding volumes are backed by real geometry before spawning.

PROBLEM 03

Mobile SoC Performance

Running Passthrough MR + Physics + AI is intensive. We staggered AI state-machine ticks and used GPU Instancing for all characters to maintain a locked 72Hz.

04 / Logic

AI State Machine

The EnemyAI Controller uses a four-state machine driven by player awareness and aggression timers. This ensures enemies feel reactive and smart in any room layout.

Behavior

Enemies peek from cover, taunt the player, and reposition if they detect a near-miss.

Interaction

Physical throws with haptic feedback and screen-edge indicators for high-tension feedback.
