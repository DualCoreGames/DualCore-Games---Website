01  /  System Architecture

The Synesthesia Layer

We developed a decoupled Audio-Visual Synesthesia Engine that bridges the gap between the audio DSP and the rendering pipeline. This ensures the environment 'breathes' with the narrative without impacting frame-timing.

Frequency Analyzer

Real-time FFT analysis of the spatialized audio stream to derive rhythmic and atmospheric data for the visual systems.

Volumetric Mixer

Lightweight volumetric fog and light-beam shaders that modulate opacity and color based on the frequency data.

Retopo Pipeline

Semi-automated workflow for transforming high-poly photogrammetry scans into mobile-performant VR assets.

02  /  Challenges & Solutions

Engineering Presence in VR

01

Spatial Audio Performance Overhead

Problem

CPU Spikes with High HRTF Count

Using high-quality HRTF (Head-Related Transfer Function) spatialization for 30+ simultaneous audio sources on a mobile SoC often led to critical CPU bottlenecks.

Solution

Adaptive Audio Culling & Thread Dispatching

We implemented a distance-based audio culling system and offloaded the spatialization calculations to the hardware-accelerated DSP cores where available, freeing up the main CPU for logic.

02

Preserving Immersion During Loads

Problem

Frame Drops and GC Hitches

In meditation, even a 1-frame hitch is enough to break 'Presence.' Background asset loading and garbage collection (GC) were causing micro-stutters during atmospheric shifts.

Solution

Incremental GC & Asset Warm-Loading

We enforced a strict zero-allocation runtime policy and moved to incremental garbage collection. All environment transitions are 'warm-loaded' into memory before the transition begins.

03  /  Engineering Takeaways

Validation & Results

1

Multisensory Sync Drives Presence

The synchronization of sight and sound is the most powerful tool for creating VR presence. When the environment breathes with the audio, the user's brain accepts the virtual space as 'real'.

2

Audio-First Optimization for VR

In simulation, high-quality spatial audio often provides a greater 'presence' ROI than high-fidelity visuals. We prioritized audio CPU headroom over heavy post-processing.

3

Photogrammetry Requires Manual Polish

While scans provide realism, they are inherently un-performant. A dedicated retopology and UV-optimization pipeline is mandatory for production-ready mobile XR assets.
