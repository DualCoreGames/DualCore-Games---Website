01  /  System Architecture

The Infinite Runner Pipeline

We engineered a Chunk-Based Generator that operates independently of the main thread. This ensures that the physics and rendering loops remain untouched while new level data is synthesized.

Deterministic RNG

Centralized seed registry that ensures identical level generation for shared challenges and global leaderboard verification.

Job-Based Physics

Custom lightweight physics wrapper that utilizes C# Job System for multi-threaded collision detection on mobile CPUs.

Object Pooler 2.0

Advanced pooling system that recycles mesh data and particle systems to eliminate runtime memory allocations (GC).

02  /  Challenges & Solutions

Engineering Procedural Smoothness

01

Chunk Loading Stutter

Problem

Frame Spikes During New Chunk Synthesis

Generating new level geometry and spawning obstacles in a single frame caused noticeable 'hitching,' which is fatal for high-speed arcade games.

Solution

Amortized Spawning & Prefab Warming

We spread the 'cost' of a new chunk over 5-10 frames and pre-warmed all necessary prefabs in the background. The generation is triggered 2-3 chunks ahead of the player view.

02

Mobile Battery & Thermal Load

Problem

High Power Consumption from Physics/VFX

The 'vibrant' aesthetic requested (many particles, dynamic collisions) caused older mobile devices to thermal-throttle within 10 minutes of play.

Solution

Vertex-Based VFX & Lightweight Math

We replaced standard pixel-heavy particles with vertex-animated meshes and simplified our physics calculations to use squared-distance checks, reducing CPU overhead by 40%.

03  /  Engineering Takeaways

Validation & Results

1

Performance is a Gameplay Mechanic

By locking the frame rate to 60 FPS, we increased player retention by 25%. A smooth experience directly correlates to higher engagement in high-velocity arcade titles.

2

Systemic Difficulty > Fixed Level Design

The "Dynamic Difficulty Scaling" algorithm allowed us to support players of all skill levels without creating hundreds of static levels, reducing content-cost significantly.

3

Mobile-First Optimization Pays Off

Optimizing for the most constrained mobile hardware ensured that the game was production-ready for global markets where high-end flagship devices are not the norm.
