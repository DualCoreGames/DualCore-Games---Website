02  /  Challenges & Solutions

Engineering Psychological Tension

01

Narrative Driven Event Sequencing

Problem

Linear Expectations in Open Environments

Because paranormal events are directly tied to diary discoveries, the system needed to support dynamic story triggers that activate environmental changes in a controlled narrative order, even when players navigate non-linearly.

Solution

Event-Driven Narrative System

Diary entries act as narrative triggers broadcasted across a decoupled event bus. This system ensures that environmental changes occur only after the player has discovered key story elements, maintaining narrative pacing seamlessly.

02

Environmental Consistency During Distortions

Problem

Visual Continuity Breakdowns

As supernatural events begin to alter the house, maintaining visual continuity while introducing subtle environmental distortions becomes critical for preserving immersion. Traditional toggling of objects caused jarring pop-ins.

Solution

Dynamic Environmental State System

Environmental objects, lighting, and audio layers are controlled through a centralized state system. This allows the house to gradually change as the story progresses, creating subtle, interpolated shifts that reinforce the supernatural influence smoothly.

03

Managing Player Expectations

Problem

Predictable "Scripted Event" Fatigue

Players exploring horror environments often anticipate scripted events or jump scares. The challenge was designing systems that make paranormal encounters feel unpredictable and psychologically unsettling rather than repetitive.

Solution

Controlled Encounters & Layered Feedback

Supernatural events are triggered through carefully timed sequences tied to exploration progress. Instead of relying solely on jump scares, the game uses layered horror cues—sound design, environmental motion, shadow behavior, and visual distortion—to create sustained tension.

03  /  Engineering Takeaways

Validation in Production

1

Narrative-Driven Systems Enhance Horror Immersion

Connecting paranormal encounters directly to story progression ensures that each supernatural event feels meaningful and reinforces the narrative experience, rather than feeling like a random sequence of events.

2

Environmental Storytelling Strengthens Player Engagement

Allowing players to uncover the story through exploration encourages curiosity and deepens immersion within the haunted environment.

3

Psychological Horror Requires Controlled Pacing

Gradual escalation of supernatural events allows tension to build naturally, making each encounter more impactful than a barrage of immediate threats.

4

Dynamic Environment Systems Support Immersive Horror

By allowing environmental elements to subtly change over time through the centralized state controller, the game creates a living space where the house itself feels like an active participant in the horror experience.
