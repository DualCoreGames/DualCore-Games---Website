01  /  System Architecture

The State-Discovery Engine

We engineered a three-tier decoupled architecture to separate narrative progression from visual execution. This allows the designers to create complex "knowledge dependencies" without hard-coding triggers into the environment.

Narrative Registry

Centralized bit-field that tracks every "Narrative Fragment" discovered by the player. Used as the ground truth for all system evaluations.

Pacing Controller

Analyzes player telemetry (speed, proximity, discovery rate) to update the real-time "Terror Metric."

Environmental Mixer

Layered component system that blends procedural shaders, spatialized audio, and lighting presets based on the current state.

02  /  Challenges & Solutions

Engineering Systemic Horror

01

Non-Linear Discovery Conflicts

Problem

State Dependencies and Sequence Breaking

Players in an open-ended environment may discover items in a different order than expected. Traditional boolean flag systems become unmanageable with 50+ discovery points and complex "if-then" requirements.

Solution

Bitwise Masking & Priority Evaluation

We moved to a bitwise state registry where environmental shifts require a specific "knowledge mask." This allows for complex dependencies (e.g., Fragment A + Fragment B, but NOT C) to be evaluated in a single CPU instruction.

02

Performance During World Distortion

Problem

Visual Frame Drops During Transitions

High-quality HDRP environmental shifts (warping geometry, changing material properties) often caused frame spikes, breaking immersion at the most critical narrative moments.

Solution

Compute Shader Warping & Material Layering

We offloaded geometric distortion to compute shaders and used a layered material approach where transition states are pre-calculated. The transition is then a simple lerp between layers, ensuring a locked 60 FPS.

03  /  Engineering Takeaways

Validation & Results

1

Systemic Horror Over Scripted Triggers

By moving from coordinate-based triggers to state-based systemic logic, we reduced the manual level-design cost by 40% while increasing player-perceived depth and reactivity.

2

Decoupling Narrative from Asset Logic

Allowing the narrative team to update the bitwise registry without touching environmental prefabs enabled parallel development and much faster iteration on story-beats.

3

Data-Driven Pacing is the Future

The "Terror Metric" proved that personalized tension is significantly more effective than static horror design. It ensures every player has a unique, optimally paced experience regardless of their playstyle.
